Scene 01

  • Date of Play: Saturday, October 21st, 2023

The sun’s glow still lightened the western sky even though it had set over an hour ago. Jaycob adjusted his sunglasses and looked to the west in annoyance. It was still too bright. The light turned green and he accelerated away, the small engine on his scooter revving to a high note.

The journey to his parent’s home was not a long one. He’d made sure that his domain was close by so he could be on hand should they need him.

Does the family have a poor image of him? He is a night owl, so Likely. Roll: 62, Yes.

Not that they ever needed him. As a Child of the Night, he only visited after the sun had set and they knew that he slept during the day. That and his father’s certainty that he was on some kind of drugs, or dealing drugs, or what-have-you made sure that no one wanted him around.

But he wanted them around. He wanted to be around them. His Embrace had been rough and with everything that came with it…

He switched on a blinker and made the right-hand turn onto the street he grew up on. He passed the Smith’s house, then the James’ house, though neither family still lived there. The neighborhood had gone downhill along with his father’s auto repair business and they hadn’t been able to flee it like their neighbors.

He motored up into the driveway, then switched off the scooter, popping the kickstand and leaping off of it in one smooth motion. He was soon at his parents door and ringing the doorbell.

Do they answer? Even odds. Roll: 72, no they don’t. Are they there? Even odds. Roll: 65, no. I bet Jaycob forgot something that the family was doing. An important something.

There was no answer at the door. He looked over his shoulder at the street where his father’s old SUV was normally parked and saw that it was gone.

“Oh, shit!” he groaned, realizing that he’d completely forgotten. Tonight was his younger sister’s graduation ceremony! The Etiquette class his mother made them all take as children had a full course leading up to a graduation where the student was deemed to be an “adult.” Jaycob had dropped out before getting to that point, but his sister hadn’t.

And she was graduating tonight. Jaycob checked his phone, looking at the time.

Does he have time to maybe get there to see the graduation? Somewhat Likely. Roll: 06, Exceptional Yes!

Yes, he could make it, he realized! He rushed back to his moped and hopped on. He had to get there on time.

Make it a Dexterity + Drive roll to get there. Because I got an Exceptional Yes on the “is there time to arrive” question, I won’t bump the difficulty up. However, I don’t have Drive, so that does bump it up one. Roll: 1 success!

He zoomed up to the front of the parking area, cutting the engine off before he was fully stopped. He let the moped fall over and was off and running towards the entrance. He made it in just as the small orchestra announced the presentation of the graduates.

He moved quietly and smoothly over to where he spotted his parents. His father’s eyes narrowed when he saw him, and his mother gave him a sour smile.

“Did you forget?” she asked him quietly as he sat next to her.

He responded with a curt nod. “Yep. Sorry.”

She eyed him up and down, taking in the ripped jeans, leather jacket and sunglasses he was still wearing. She turned away without further comment.

He ripped the glasses off his face. “Sorry,” he offered again in a quiet voice.

Does anything interesting happen during the presentation? Somewhat Likely. Roll: 22, Yes. I don’t know what it might be, so let’s roll on the Event chart: Change / Reality. Interesting. Since this isn’t the Matrix, I don’t think things will shift completely. But, his reality needs to shift completely. And his reality is that he’s keeping both worlds separate.

Then, something catches his eye. A face that he almost recognizes. His gaze whips back to where he thought he saw it and…

Rolling Perception + Alertness. Roll: 2 successes.

Yes! There it was, the face! The face of Ming Ping. She smiled at him, then glanced meaningfully over at his sister, then waggled her eyebrows.

Jaycob knew fear in that moment. What was she doing here? And, what did she plan to do?

Is Ming Ping an enemy of Jaycob? Somewhat Likely. Roll: 64, Yes.

Making eye contact, he nodded towards the exit. She raised an eyebrow.

Rolling Appearance + Expression to convince her to go outside with me. Roll: 2 successes.

Jaycob nodded towards the exit again. This time Ming Ping smiled demurely and stood, making her way there. Jaycob turned to his mother and whispered, “Sorry, mom, I have to leave. Something’s come up. But give Jessica my congratulations!”

Without waiting for her, he stood and left.

He offered her a shrug. “My mom made us all attend the school.”

Ming Ping laughed softly. “My my, you are young!” Jaycob realized that he’d given away information that he might have wanted to keep hidden.

Not that he would have been able to keep it hidden very long. He felt a weight settle on his shoulders. He had to keep them safe, his family safe.

“Can I trust you to keep my secret?” he asked, knowing full well that he couldn’t.

Ming Ping’s eyebrow quirked up. “Can you, my young friend?”

He sighed. “What is it you want?”

She gave him a once over, pursing her lips in thought. “Honestly, I don’t know what you can offer me,” she admitted. “But, I’m willing to receive a boon from you.” He lips quirked up again. “You never know when it might be useful to have someone such as you on my side.”

Now Jaycob owes Ming Ping a Minor Boon for keeping his secret. Hooray for the Jyhad!

Jaycob nodded quickly. “Fine. You know how to get a hold of me, I assume?”

Her smile turned impish. “Oh, yes. With a sire like yours, we all keep tabs on you.”

That was a revelation that Jaycob didn’t like. While he had assumed that was the case, having it confirmed for him was not pleasant. He nodded to her, and then she turned and walked away.

Jaycob stared after her, fear bubbling inside him. Not fear for himself, but for his family. He had to keep them safe.

End of this scene. Chaos bumps up to 7.

Scene 03

Jaycob’s scooter buzzed up to Fernanda’s office. It was small, but not because Fernanda was unsuccessful, but because that’s all she needed. She was a club promoter, and one of the best in the business. One word from her would make or break a club.

Ice water bathed Jaycob’s veins. Ming Ping had gotten to Fernanda first. What did that meant?

It meant she was going to be keeping a very close eye on him.

He gritted his teeth in frustration. Fernanda noticed and asked him what was wrong.

“Hamid’s boss is a…” he paused to find the right word. “A rival of mine, I suppose.”

Fernanda looked surprised. “Really,” she said in a disbelieving tone. “I wouldn’t think you would run in the same circles.”

Rolling Charisma + Expression to convince her of the truth of this. Roll: 2 successes.

“Well, we do,” he said. “Kind of.” He shrugged. “Suffice it to say she’s a powerful woman and I owe her a favor. And, because of that, she’s keeping a very close eye on me.”

Fernanda smiled. “Okay, I can buy that.” She looked thoughtful, then leaned forward. “Do you think that’s why Hamid showed up tonight?” Jaycob nodded.

Fernanda’s smile grew. “Well, you need to start owing powerful people more favors. I could use a lot more contracts like the one they’re offering!”

Jaycob had to smile at that, but he didn’t feel the humor.

“Well,” he said slowly, “if you’re working closely with her, perhaps you can help me keep tabs on her.”

Rolling Manipulation + Expression to convince her to do this. Roll: 0 successes.

Fernanda started shaking her head before Jaycob had even finished speaking. “No, I won’t spy on her for you,” she said forcefully. “I’m not going to get involved in it.” She shrugged. “I have to look out for myself, you know?”

She must have seen something in Jaycob’s expression, because she amended, “I’ll let you know if I hear anything that could be dangerous for you, but only that.”

Jaycob nodded, “I guess that’s the best I can ask. Thank you.”
